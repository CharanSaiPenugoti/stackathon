DEPRESSION

Depression is a serious condition that negatively affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. In contrast to normal sadness, clinical depression is persistent, often interferes with a person’s ability to experience or anticipate pleasure, and significantly interferes with functioning in daily life. Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. Also called major depressive disorder or clinical depression, it affects how you feel, think and behave and can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems. You may have trouble doing normal day-to-day activities, and sometimes you may feel as if life isn't worth living.
SYMPTOMS

Although depression may occur only once during your life, people typically have multiple episodes. During these episodes, symptoms occur most of the day, nearly every day and may include:

  1. Feelings of sadness, tearfulness, emptiness or hopelessness
  2. .Angry outbursts, irritability or frustration, even over small matters
  3. Loss of interest or pleasure in most or all normal activities, such as sex, hobbies or sports
  4. Sleep disturbances, including insomnia or sleeping too much
  5. Tiredness and lack of energy, so even small tasks take extra effort
  6. Reduced appetite and weight loss or increased cravings for food and weight gain
  7. Anxiety, agitation or restlessness
  8. Slowed thinking, speaking or body movements.Feelings of worthlessness or guilt, fixating on past failures or self-blame
  9. Trouble thinking, concentrating, making decisions and remembering things
  10. Frequent or recurrent thoughts of death, suicidal thoughts, suicide attempts or suicide.Unexplained physical problems, such as back pain or headaches.

Types of Depression and Ayurvedic Treatment for Depression :

1.Pitta-Type Depression

Evoking pitta can lead to pitta-type depression in your life. This kind of mental imbalance typically originates from a sense of disappointment, stemming from events such as losing a job or getting divorced. Symptoms of this pitta-type depression incorporates

  • Excessive criticism, both towards the self and others
  • Irritability,A sense of failure
  • Accusing others or Accusing themselves
  • Anger, outrage and aggression
  • Feeling useless,Guilt
  • Hiding your true feelings from others,Suicidal thoughts
Treatment :

Finding a way to reduce pitta depression in your life is the way to conquer this condition. This should be possible by rehearsing relaxation techniques like breathing exercises, yoga, or meditation. Other choices incorporate avoiding excessively hot, zesty, and light food varieties. Instead of that, favor gentle, dry, and cooling food sources like beans, potatoes, oats, and most vegetables.

2.Vata-Type Depression

You are bound to experience vata-type depression if your diet or daily routine contains too much vata. This mental health disorder is caused by trauma, shock or loss. Symptoms that describes vata-type depression include

  • Crying easily
  • Insomnia,Reduced libido
  • Unpredictable mood swings
  • Auditory hallucinations
  • Fear and anxiety,Restlessness
  • Decreased appetite and weight loss
  • Joint aches and headache.Excessive talking
Treatment :

To Treat this kind of depression you need to avoid vata-provoking diet and lifestyle choices. Along this, attempt to stay away from crude food varieties, just as harsh, astringent, and impactful flavors. Instead, favor cooked food varieties with sweet,salty, and sour flavors. It’s additionally best to adhere to the same food routine.

3.Kapha-Type Depression

When there is an imbalance of kapha in one’s diet or lifestyle, the Kapha-type depression is born. Some symptoms of this condition include

  • Feeling down, dull, or sluggish
  • Overeating to compensate for difficult feelings,General apathy
  • Speaking very little,Sleeping too much
  • Weight gain
  • Negative thought patterns.

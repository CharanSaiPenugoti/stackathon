Science of yoga and ayurveda is subtler than the science of medicine, because science of medicine is often victim of statistical manipulation. The purpose of science is not down-scaling everything to physical level and measure, but to penetrate deeper into the realm beyond the sensory perceptions and bring more in-depth knowledge and wisdom to the world.
“Physical illness” (vs mental illness) isn’t really a precise medical term— not like “cancer” or “metabolic disease” or “autoimmune disease.” It’s a term doctors use a lot as a shorthand in casual speech, but it’s not a diagnostic category. When doctors say “physical illness” what we mean, generally is “an identifiable problem in the tissues of the body that causes the symptoms you’re having.” If you have abdominal pain because of a stomach ulcer, that’s something we can test for and see. It’s something we have a reliable treatment for— we can cure it. So we call it a “physical illness.”
